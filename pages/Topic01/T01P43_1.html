Septic Shock
There is NO acute blood loss
  • 10 mL/kg normal saline (NS)
  • IV, UVC, or IO
  • Infusion time:
  • 30 minutes or more rapid for severe shock Repeat NS bolus as necessary
  • Positive response to treatment:
  • Less tachycardia
  • Improved CRT
  • Declining lactate
  • Improved pH
  • Improved BP
There IS acute blood loss
  • Packed red blood cells (PRBCs) While waiting for PRBCs, give 10 mL/kg NS
  • Infusion time:
  • PRBCs over 30 minutes to 2 hours
  • More rapid if severely anemic
  • Positive response to treatment:
  • Less tachycardia
  • Improved CRT
  • Declining lactate
  • Improved pH
  • Improved BP
  • Improved oxygenation
History of chronic blood loss   infant may not tolerate volume boluses. Consult a neonatal expert if in doubt about whether to administer volume or how much to give.
If possible, obtain the newborn screen prior to giving a blood transfusion, but do not delay giving a transfusion if needed emergently.
