Peripheral Perfusion
  • Capillary refill time > 3 seconds
  • Cool extremities
  • Mottled skin
Pulses
  • Weak
  • Compare brachial to femoral
    • If brachial is stronger than femoral, evaluate for ductal dependent, left-outflow tract obstructive congenital heart disease (such as coarctation of the aorta).
  • Bounding
    • If present, assess for wide pulse pressure.
Respiratory and Blood Gas
  • Tachypnea
  • Increased work of breathing
  • Apnea
  • Gasping
  • Metabolic acidosis or mixed metabolic/respiratory acidosis
  • Rising lactate level
Color
  • Cyanosis
    • Severe hypoxemia worsens anaerobic metabolism.
  • Pale or white skin (reflection of anemia)
    • Low O2 content in blood – tissue hypoxia
Neurologic Status
  • May initially be irritable
  • Lethargic, decreased level of consciousness
  • Hypotonic or hypertonic
  • Abnormal, depressed reflexes (Moro, hand grasp, suck, gag, and deep tendon reflexes)
  • Coma
  • Seizures
Heart
  • Size
    • Enlarged
    • Small or compressed
  • Shape
    • If abnormal, could be a sign of CHD
  • Location
    • Displaced
    • Dextrocardia
  • Murmur
  • Muffled heart sounds
Heart Rate
  • Tachycardia – may reflect poor cardiac output
  • Bradycardia – hypoxemia and acidosis can depress conduction system
  • Arrhythmia
Blood Pressure
  • Normal
  • Low (hypotensive)
  • Evaluate pulse pressure
    • Narrow – heart failure with low systemic output, cardiac tamponade, hyperinflation, and pneumothorax
    • Wide – aortic runoff lesions (patent ductus arteriosus with left-to-right shunt, truncus arteriosus, arteriovenous malformation)
Urine Output
  • Less than 1 mL/kg per hour or declining
