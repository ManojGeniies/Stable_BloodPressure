Respiratory Rate and Effort
O2 Saturation, Acid-Base Status, and Color
 
 
  • Evaluate the O2 saturation and blood gas to determine oxygenation and acid-base status.
  • Severe hypoxemia will worsen anaerobic metabolism.
  •  
    Pale or white skin color may indicate a low hemoglobin secondary to hemorrhage.
    In infants with darker skin tones, assess mucous membranes for pale color.
  • With severe anemia, O2 content of the blood will be low and will be an additional cause of tissue hypoxia.
